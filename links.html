Bryce Resources
Most of the generator programs mentioned in Cruising the Canyon were found on a blog by Horo, which also has many other tools as wells as documentations for Bryce 3D, as well as other 3D goodies. There are many more tools listed on his as well.

POV-Ray’s website also have a multitude of different programs for various applications in their Resources: Links section.

If you're looking for more Bryce tutorials, here are some great resources:
  • David Brinnen on YouTube. He has a ton of tutorials of varying skill level. The beginner videos explain concepts in a very understandable manner, and the expert videos are complex but thorough.
  • Bryce Discussion Forums. The forum is frequented by experts of the program and many threads are useful as references and guides. This discussion links to a bunch of useful threads.
  • bryce-tutorials.info is a website that has a lot of free and paid assets. In addition, its tutorial section is basically a compilation of hundreds of tutorials from various people.
  • robinwood.com also has useful beginner and intermediate tutorials revolving around different parts of Bryce.
If you are looking for inspiration you’ll find that Bryce content can be found pretty much anywhere. Reddit and Instagram are pretty popular with Bryce art, though you can generally find it on any social media platform. If you particularly want animations, searching “Bryce 3D animation” on YouTube will bring up heaps of animations. Inspiration is out there, you just have to find it!
